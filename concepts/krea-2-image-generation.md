# Krea 2 Image Generation

Krea 2 is a foundation model for image generation, trained from scratch with a focus on aesthetic quality and fine-grained control. Unlike models built on top of existing architectures, Krea was designed with generation quality as the primary objective.

## Key Capabilities

### Aesthetic Foundation
Trained from scratch rather than fine-tuned from an existing model. The result is a model where aesthetic coherence isn't bolted on — it's native to the architecture.

### Fine Control Features
- **Style transfer** — apply specific visual styles to generated content
- **Moodboard input** — feed reference images to guide the aesthetic direction
- **Adjustable creativity** — dial creativity up or down depending on whether you need precision or exploration

### Multi-Platform Availability
Available via multiple AI platforms including Hermes, Claude, Codex, and OpenClaw — making it a cross-platform creative tool rather than a single-platform feature.

## Why It Matters for Creative Work

Image generation has largely been dominated by models optimized for photorealism or broad capability coverage. Krea 2's differentiator is **aesthetic intentionality** — the model is explicitly trained to produce visually coherent, stylistically controllable outputs.

For creative workflows:
- Moodboard input means you can anchor generation to visual references without describing them in text
- Style transfer means aesthetic consistency across a series of outputs
- Adjustable creativity means you can toggle between precise control and generative exploration

## Relationship to Personal Knowledge Management

Aesthetic quality matters in knowledge work too — how information is presented affects how it's received. Tools like [[personal-operating-system]] show that Obsidian-based personal OS systems benefit from visual coherence. Krea 2 represents a similar principle at the generation layer: don't just produce images, produce images with intentional aesthetic direction.
