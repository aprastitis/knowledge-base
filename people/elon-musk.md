# Elon Musk — Management Heuristics

## Type
People / Leadership Framework

## Summary
Beyond the 5-step algorithm, Elon Musk applies additional management principles around ownership, hiring, failure, and decision-making. These heuristics complement the 5-step process and apply broadly to how to run organizations, teams, and technical work.

## Manager Hands-On Rule

> "All technical managers must have hands-on experience. For example, managers of software teams must spend at least 20% of their time coding. Solar roof managers must spend time on the roofs doing installations. Otherwise, they are like a cavalry leader who can't ride a horse or a general who can't use a sword."

**Application:** If you're a manager who hasn't done the technical work recently, you can't judge the work of your team. You become a bureaucrat instead of a leader.

## Failure Philosophy

> "It's OK to be wrong. Just don't be confident and wrong."

**Application:** Confidence without competence is dangerous. It's fine to try and fail — it's not fine to be confidently wrong and keep pushing.

## Leadership Rule

> "Never ask your troops to do something you're not willing to do."

**Application:** If you won't do it yourself, don't send others to do it. This is the ultimate test of whether a task is worth delegating.

## The Only Real Rules

> "The only rules are the ones dictated by the laws of physics. Everything else is a recommendation."

**Application:** Most "rules" in organizations are convention, not physics. Challenge them accordingly. If someone says "we can't do that because of physics" — that's a fact. If they say "we can't do that because it's not how it's done" — that's a recommendation.

## Hiring Rule

> "When hiring, look for people with the right attitude. Skills can be taught. Attitude requires a brain transplant."

**Application:** Attitude (growth mindset, ownership, resilience) predicts long-term success more than skills. Skills can be learned; fundamental attitude is much harder to change.

## Problem-Solving Rule (Skip-Level Meetings)

> "Whenever there are problems to solve, don't just meet with your managers. Do a skip level, where you meet with the right below your managers."

**Application:** Middle management filters information and can insulate leadership from reality. Go directly to the people doing the work to get accurate information.
